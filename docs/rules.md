# Skout — Official Rules Reference

Skout is a ladder-climbing card game where players take on the role of circus leaders. The goal is to create the most impressive show by "Showing" sets of cards or "Skouting" cards from rivals.

---

## 1. Deck & Setup

### Dual-Indexed Cards

- Every card has two values (top and bottom).

> **No Reordering**: You must never change the order of cards in your hand.

- **Orientation Choice**: At the start of each round, you may choose to use your hand exactly as dealt or flip it 180° to use the reverse values. This orientation is fixed for the entire round.

### Deck Composition

| Players | Cards Removed | Cards per Hand |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 2 Players | Remove the 9/10 card | 11 |
| 3 Players | Remove all cards with a 10 | 12 |
| 4 Players | Remove the 9/10 card | 11 |
| 5 Players | Use all 45 cards | 9 |

---

## 2. Turn Actions

On your turn, you must perform **exactly one** of the following:

### A. SHOW (Play a Set)

- Play a set of **adjacent cards** from your hand.
- The set must **beat** the current Active Set on the table.
- **Victory**: If you beat the set, you take the old set into your score pile (each card = 1 point). Your new set becomes the Active Set.

### B. SKOUT (Take a Card)

- Take one card from **either end** of the current Active Set.
- **Placement**: Place it anywhere in your hand, in either orientation.
- **Compensation**: Pay **1 Skout Chip** to the owner of the Active Set.
- **Empty Table**: If you Skout the **last card** of an Active Set, the table becomes empty. The next player may then "Show" any cards they want (they get a "free play").

### C. SKOUT & SHOW (Combo)

- Once per round, you may use your **Skout & Show chip**.
- Perform a Skout action, then immediately perform a Show action.
- The Show must beat the remaining cards on the table (if any).

> **2-Player Variant**: The Skout & Show action is **unavailable** in 2-player games.

---

## 3. Card Set Strength

Sets are compared in this order of precedence:

1. **Quantity**: A set with more cards always beats a set with fewer.
2. **Type**: **Matching sets** (e.g., 5-5-5) are stronger than **Consecutive sets** (e.g., 4-5-6) of the same size.
3. **Value**: If size and type are identical, the set with the **highest minimum value** wins.
    - *Example*: A matching set of `9-9` beats `7-7`. A run of `4-5-6` beats `1-2-3`.

---

## 4. Round End

A round ends **immediately** when:

1. **Empty Hand**: A player plays their last card.
2. **Return to Owner**: A player's Active Set remains on the field until it is their turn again (i.e., all other players skouted or could not act).

---

## 5. Scoring

At the end of the round, calculate your score:

- **Captured Cards**: Each card in your score pile = **+1 Point**.
- **Skout Chips**: Each Skout chip you received = **+1 Point**.
- **Remaining Cards**: Each card left in your hand = **-1 Point**.

> **EXEMPTION**: If you ended the round with an **unbeatable set** (Condition #2), you do **NOT** receive penalties for the cards in your hand.
